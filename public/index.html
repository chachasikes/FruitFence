Fruit Fence

Fruit fences are clip on planters for city fences.

Features

  • Use existing city infrastructure Clipping a planter to a fence allows us to start growing fruits vertically, in spaces that would normally be inactive, or even ugly.
  • City Fruits for City Microclimates Our first plantings for the Bay Area are of blueberries, strawberries and lemon trees.
  • Easy Pattern The Fruit Fence Bags can be easily sewn with a normal sewing machine. A good project for beginning stitchers.
  • Low-cost Materials Order or Upcycle Tyvek (or similar material), and plants are the main cost. Add some fun digital accessories to your project for added fun.
  • Transparent Food Production A printable surface allows the community effort of fruit care to be more educational. The more we understand about fruiting plants, the better we can advocate for stronger regional fruit production
  • Lights up at night LED lights in the water reservoir become extra lighting for darker urban spaces.
  • Fun to water A self-watering system can be watered from the outside, and movement in the water will animate fruit scenes, making it fun to add water to the reservoir at night. Not watering the plant directly helps to protect the health of the plant, which can be maintained by a community of volunteers.
  • Community Art Projects A Fruit Fence is a perfect community art project. Creative activities can be done in a workshop, and can easily integrate with lessons about electronics, plant biology, graphic design, and the physics of water.
  • Community Care via Social Media We have a system for community caring for shared fruit planters that uses Twitter.
